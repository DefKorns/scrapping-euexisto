Alone with everybody


the flesh covers the bone  
and they put a mind  
in there and  
sometimes a soul,  
and the women break  
vases against the walls  
and the men drink too  
much  
and nobody finds the  
one  
but keep  
looking  
crawling in and out  
of beds.  
flesh covers  
the bone and the  
flesh searches  
for more than  
flesh.  

there's no chance  
at all:  
we are all trapped  
by a singular  
fate.  

nobody ever finds  
the one.  

the city dumps fill  
the junkyards fill  
the madhouses fill  
the hospitals fill  
the graveyards fill  

nothing else  
fills. 

Charles Bukowski
